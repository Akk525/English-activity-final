Colour schemes


  1. Yellow is the colour of the national geography logo. The yellow border around the magazine also resembles the national geography logo.

  2. The colour yellow can stand for intellect which can represent National Geographic as a symbol of knowledge.

  3. Yellow is also refered to the sun which shows that the magazine will be related to nature in some way. (National Geographic is also a magazine for wildlife and nature)

  4. Yellow is a vibrant colour and can easily grasp the attention of the passer-by similar the one around this section section.

  5. The masthead, headline and caption use colours which can be clearly distinguished from the background, making them standout from the rest of the magazine.

  6. Using red for the date at the top creates a high contrast with the yellow background making it prominent.

Typography


  1. Uppercase letters for the masthead, teasers and headline almost commands the user to look at and read the magazine as uppercase characters can connote shouting.

  2. Certain words have a thicker font then others to grab the attention of the user and make them curious. For example the word Mars stands out from the rest of the headline and makes the reader curious about the contenst of the magazine.

  3. The magazine uses two kinds of fonts: serif and sans-serif. Serif fonts give away the feeling of authority. They connote that the establishment is old and experienced in their field which happens to be the case with National Geographic. Sans-Serif fonts are said to be more modern and casual. In this case, the headline uses this font to make the magazine more appealling to the youth.

  4. The masthead, headline and caption use colurs which can be clearly distinguished from the background, making them standout from the rest of the magazine.

Positioning


  1. Teasers in the ears of the magazine give additional insight about the contents of the magazine.

  2. The headline and title wrap around the image and in the process put it on the spotlight.

  3. The angle at which the picture of the rover was taken in creates the impression of the viewer directly looking down at the rover.

  4. The yellow border around the image frames it and makes it seem like we are looking through a window.

  5. The frame combined with the angle of the image make it seem that we too can travel to Mars upon reading the magazine.
